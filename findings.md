# Major Findings

## 1. Call duration is the strongest signal

Subscribers averaged **537 seconds** per call vs. **223 seconds** for non-subscribers — a 2.4x gap. Longer, more engaged conversations track closely with conversion. This is the single most predictive behavioral indicator in the dataset.

## 2. Cellular outreach outperforms other channels

**82.6%** of subscribers were reached via cellular vs. **62.5%** of non-subscribers, who saw roughly 3x more "unknown" contact records. This points to both a channel-effectiveness gap and a data-quality/reachability issue for the non-subscriber group.

## 3. Household debt load matters

**63.4%** of subscribers carry no housing loan, versus only **43.0%** of non-subscribers. Existing loan obligations appear to reduce appetite for committing to a new term deposit. The pattern is mirrored (and inverted) for clients who do carry a housing loan: 36.59% of subscribers vs. 56.97% of non-subscribers.

## 4. Occupation shapes conversion likelihood

Blue-collar clients are over-represented among non-subscribers (**21.05%** vs. **13.39%** of subscribers) — the largest gap of any occupation category. Management remains the top segment in both groups, and retired clients convert at a notably higher rate (9.76% share of subscribers) than they decline (4.46% share of non-subscribers).

## 5. Prior campaign success predicts future success

Clients with a successful previous campaign outcome show a far higher share among subscribers than non-subscribers, whose previous-outcome distribution is dominated by "unknown" and "failure" results. Prior engagement history is a strong repeat-conversion signal.

## 6. May is the seasonal peak

Both groups saw their highest contact volume in May, but non-subscribers were far more concentrated in a handful of months (May, Jul, Aug, Jun) than subscribers, whose volume is spread more evenly across the year — suggesting that timing alone isn't sufficient to convert a client.
